About us

We empower gyms and trainers to connect with and develop those with mobility problems or disabilities to be the type of person that would never invite sympathy. We offer training systems and live seminars to educate coaches on inexpensive Modifications, to their existing training. We help your gym become Adaptive. You and your program become leaders to help others Overcome any challenge.

Seminars

Our in person seminars help you and your staff to include those of all abilities and functional levels. We help apply our subject matter expertise to your passion for delivering the best to your community.

Online Courses

Our excellent online 3-part video training system is a simple way to help movement trainers modify exercises so that everyone may participate safely and without extra burden upon coaches.

Curriculum

We offer a digital based curriculum for you and your staff to complete without interruption to your operations. Gain skills and knowledge at your own availbility.

The Founder

Morgan

Founder

"What a dynamo! Caring and knowledgeable." - Larry Halperin

Hello, my name is Morgan Fielder and I love to lift heavy-ish things. My life's work includes acting as a sedentary lifestyle slayer, physical therapist, teacher trainer, and broker between imperfect athletes of all ages and neighborhood fitness programs. I also obsessively think and talk about good food. When not working as a disability ninja, you will find me drinking coffee and discussing something silly with my husband, spartan racing with my family, binge watching fantasy soap operas, or attending a potluck.

Read More

While a student at the United States Naval Academy I majored in Economics because I wanted to become a Top Gun/investment banker type or maybe a female Magnum P.I. Thankfully I discovered art, music, literature and dancing (much thanks to my future hubby and favorite professor) and decided not to crush Wall Street but set my sights on a career of service. After four years of ship driving and managing engine rooms, I decided the best way for me to help others was through physical therapy because I always loved sports and blood grosses me out.

I spent the better part of two decades running competetively (with some dabbling in yoga, pilates, and rugby), living in fantastic places like New England, San Diego, and Seoul, marrying one of the best Good Dudes (GD) on the planet, and playing furiously with my two gorgeous girls, and acting as "garden mom" for the school PTA. My career is pretty rad. I get to help people through injuries, rehabilitate the elderly, and truly make lasting impact on people's lives as a scientist and a healer, with minimal time spent on the computer (bonus!). In the spring of 2014 I finally succumbed to the "crossfit craze".

I had avoided it for years, but after learning to do a legit pullup in my middle thirties and comments from others about my "rocking bod", I was hooked! I have always been very active, but other modes of exercise never resulted in such a transformation. For me, group weight training made me feel physically powerful, emotionally stable, and part of a really excellent community of like minded people. In the meantime, I had always wanted to become an entrepreneur in order to really help people make positive changes on a large scale, and in a preventative way to avoid the ravages of avoidable disease caused by poor lifestyle which our medical system fails us.

After being inspired by the unique energy and people surrounding me in the beach village of Leucadia, CA, my husband and I decided to marry our combined military experience, strength and conditioning coaching, athletic backgrounds, and my physical therapy expertise to create Modify Adapt Overcome (MAO). MAO is an online community and group of resources to help as many people as possible avoid the negative effects of a sedetary lifestyle and improve their quality of life. We believe people with mobility problems (such as amputation, spinal cord injury, being wheelchair-bound, etc) and lifestyle problems that limit mobility (such as obesity, osteoarthrtis, joint replacement etc) deserve the same access to local, neighborhood group fitness and strengthening programs as everyone else.

MAO aims to train 1000 physical trainers on how to integrate those with mobility problems into their already existing programs with minimal amount of effort. MAO will provide online solutions for gyms to learn simple exercise Modifications and implement easy physical Adaptations so that any challenge may be Overcome. Live seminars and events will be conducted to foster the growth of this community that will open up a huge customer segment for gym owners and trainers that previously were too intimidated to even step foot in the door.
